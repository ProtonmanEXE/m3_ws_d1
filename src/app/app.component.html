Welcome to an introduction to Burn!

A variety of decks make up the Modern meta of Magic: The Gathering, with some of the slower control builds like Esper or combo decks like Scapeshift having strong representation. But faster decks are available as well, such as the artifact-loving Affinity deck. The burn deck in particular is famous for its blistering speed.

Burn decks are fairly straightforward, but they are not always as simple "play burn spells and win." It is true that burn spells are the main card type in the deck, hence its name, but they can be a bit more nuanced than that. It all starts with the deck's construction and careful selection of its sideboard cards.

The Game Plan

The name of the game is to use spells which deal three (also known as "bolt to the face") or more damage to the opponent to deplete their life total from twenty to zero as fast as possible. This plan is supported by a bevy of one drops such as Goblin Guide and Monastery Swiftspear to push the damage in the early game.
